Dive Into Facts About Taniti

Frequently Asked Questions

Taniti uses 120-volt power outlets, the same as in the United States.

Alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 AM. The drinking age is 18, but enforcement is not strict.

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, English is rarely spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.

There is one hospital and several clinics on the island. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Violent crime is very rare in Taniti. However, with increased tourism, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and petty theft.

Taniti has many national holidays. On these days, some tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed. Visitors should plan accordingly.

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Currency exchange services are available at banks, and most businesses accept major credit cards.
